Improper Fractions

When it comes to fractions, we can see them big and small. We can even convert the bigger fractions into the smaller fractions. But what if we run across a fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator? These are called Improper fractions.

What do Improper Fractions look like?

Here are a few examples of some fractions: 9⁄5, 11⁄3, 3⁄3

Mixed Numbers

When it comes to improper fractions, most questions will ask you to simplify the improper fractions. When asked to, most of the time, the end result will be a mixed number.When it comes to mixed numbers, they consist of a whole number and a proper fraction.
