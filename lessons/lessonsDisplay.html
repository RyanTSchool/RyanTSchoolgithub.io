Engineering Math Prerequisites

Algebra

Algebra involves the study of equations and functions, providing a basis for solving various mathematical problems essential in engineering calculations.

Learn Algebra

Trigonometry

Trigonometry focuses on relationships between the sides and angles of triangles, which are critical in understanding waves, oscillations, and other engineering phenomena.

Learn Trigonometry

Geometry

Geometry covers the properties of shapes, sizes, and spaces, which are key in designing and analyzing structures and machinery.

Learn Geometry

Precalculus

Precalculus helps bridge the gap between algebra and calculus, with concepts like complex numbers, series, and analytic geometry being crucial for higher-level studies.

Learn Precalculus

Calculus

Calculus helps engineers model and analyze change. It includes both differentiation and integration, used for solving dynamic systems and problems in physics.

Learn Calculus

Linear Algebra

Linear algebra involves vector spaces and matrices, useful in many engineering applications like signal processing and structural analysis.

Learn Linear Algebra

Differential Equations

Differential equations describe dynamic systems and phenomena, enabling engineers to model physical processes like fluid flow and electrical circuits.

Learn Differential Equations

Statistics and Probability

Statistics and probability are crucial in analyzing data and managing uncertainty in engineering design and decision-making.

Learn Statistics and Probability

Numerical Methods

Numerical methods provide computational techniques for approximating complex problems, essential for solving engineering challenges in the real world.

Learn Numerical Methods

Discrete Mathematics

Discrete mathematics covers logic, combinatorics, graph theory, and algorithms, foundational for understanding optimization and computer science in engineering.

Learn Discrete Mathematics
